# Part 2: Sarf (Morphology — Roots, Patterns & Conjugation)

> "If Nahw tells you what a word is doing in the sentence, Sarf tells you where the word came from and how it was built. Sarf is the DNA of Arabic."

**Prerequisite:** Complete Part 1 (Phases 1-3) before starting Sarf.

---

## Chapter 1: The Root System

### The Big Idea

Almost every word in the Quran traces back to a **three-letter root** (called a جذر / jadhr or أصل / asl). These three letters carry a **core meaning**, and different patterns applied to those letters create different but related words.

### Example: The Root ك-ت-ب (K-T-B)

Core meaning: **writing**

| Pattern Applied | Word | Meaning |
|----------------|------|---------|
| فَعَلَ | كَتَبَ | He wrote |
| يَفْعُلُ | يَكْتُبُ | He writes |
| فَاعِل | كَاتِب | Writer (one who writes) |
| مَفْعُول | مَكْتُوب | Written (thing that was written) |
| فِعَال | كِتَاب | Book |
| مَفْعَل | مَكْتَب | Desk / Office (place of writing) |

**One root, many words — all connected by meaning.**

### Example: The Root ع-ل-م ('A-L-M)

Core meaning: **knowledge**

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| عَلِمَ | He knew |
| يَعْلَمُ | He knows |
| عِلْم | Knowledge |
| عَالِم | Scholar (one who knows) |
| مَعْلُوم | Known |
| عَلَّمَ | He taught (made someone know) |
| مُعَلِّم | Teacher |
| تَعَلَّمَ | He learned (sought to know) |

### How to Find the Root

**Step 1:** Remove any prefixes (ال, يَ, تَ, مُ, etc.)
**Step 2:** Remove any suffixes (ون, ين, ات, ة, etc.)
**Step 3:** Remove any extra letters that aren't part of the core
**Step 4:** You should be left with **three letters**

> **Example:** مُسْلِمُونَ
> Remove مُ (prefix) → سْلِمُونَ
> Remove ونَ (suffix) → سْلِم
> Root = **س-ل-م** (peace/submission)

### The 10 Extra Letters

When a word has more than three letters, the extra letters come from a set of 10 letters captured in the mnemonic:

**سَأَلْتُمُونِيهَا**
("You all asked me for it")

Letters: **س أ ل ت م و ن ي ه ا**

If a letter in the word is one of these 10 and removing it still leaves a meaningful 3-letter root, it is likely an extra letter.

## Chapter 2: The Verb Families (أوزان الفعل)

### The Big Idea

Arabic verbs are organized into **families** (also called forms, patterns, or أوزان / awzaan). Each family takes a 3-letter root and adds a specific pattern that gives it a predictable meaning shift.

The base family is **Family 1** (فَعَلَ). Families 2-10 are called **Thulathi Mazeed** (3-letter root with additions).

### The 10 Families

| Family | Past Pattern | Present Pattern | Meaning Shift | Example (root: ع-ل-م) |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|---------------|----------------------|
| **1** | فَعَلَ | يَفْعَلُ / يَفْعِلُ / يَفْعُلُ | Base meaning | عَلِمَ — He knew |
| **2** | فَعَّلَ | يُفَعِّلُ | Intensify / Cause | عَلَّمَ — He taught |
| **3** | فَاعَلَ | يُفَاعِلُ | Doing with someone | عَالَمَ — (not common for this root) |
| **4** | أَفْعَلَ | يُفْعِلُ | Cause / Make happen | أَعْلَمَ — He informed |
| **5** | تَفَعَّلَ | يَتَفَعَّلُ | Reflexive of 2 | تَعَلَّمَ — He learned |
| **6** | تَفَاعَلَ | يَتَفَاعَلُ | Mutual / Pretend | — |
| **7** | اِنْفَعَلَ | يَنْفَعِلُ | Passive | — |
| **8** | اِفْتَعَلَ | يَفْتَعِلُ | Reflexive | — |
| **9** | اِفْعَلَّ | يَفْعَلُّ | Colors / Defects | — |
| **10** | اِسْتَفْعَلَ | يَسْتَفْعِلُ | To seek / consider | اِسْتَغْفَرَ — He sought forgiveness |

### The Most Important Families for Quran

Families **1, 2, 4, 5, 8, and 10** appear most frequently. Let's look at them with the root **س-ل-م**:

| Family | Word | Meaning |
|--------|------|---------|
| 1 | سَلِمَ | He was safe |
| 2 | سَلَّمَ | He greeted (said salaam) / He surrendered |
| 4 | أَسْلَمَ | He submitted (to God) → became Muslim |
| 5 | تَسَلَّمَ | He received / took possession |
| 8 | اِسْتَلَمَ | He received |
| 10 | اِسْتَسْلَمَ | He surrendered completely |

### Meaning Patterns to Memorize

| Family | Common Meaning Shift | Memory Aid |
|--------|---------------------|------------|
| 2 (فَعَّلَ) | Make someone do it / do it a lot | **Doubled middle letter = extra effort** |
| 4 (أَفْعَلَ) | Cause it to happen | **أ at front = "I cause"** |
| 5 (تَفَعَّلَ) | Do it to yourself | **تَ = yourself + doubled = effort on self** |
| 8 (اِفْتَعَلَ) | Struggle / do it for yourself | **Extra تَ in the middle = internal struggle** |
| 10 (اِسْتَفْعَلَ) | Seek it / consider it | **اِسْتَ = "I'm seeking"** |

## Chapter 3: The Sarf Swatch (Conjugation Tables)

### The Big Idea

Every verb can be conjugated for **14 combinations** of person, gender, and number. These conjugations follow **predictable patterns**. The traditional method is to memorize them rhythmically — this is called the **Sarf Swatch** (تصريف).

### The Past Tense Swatch (Family 1: فَعَلَ)

Using the root ن-ص-ر (to help):

| # | Person | Arabic | Transliteration | Meaning |
|---|--------|--------|----------------|---------|
| 1 | He | نَصَرَ | nasara | He helped |
| 2 | They two (m) | نَصَرَا | nasaraa | They two helped |
| 3 | They (m) | نَصَرُوا | nasaroo | They helped |
| 4 | She | نَصَرَتْ | nasarat | She helped |
| 5 | They two (f) | نَصَرَتَا | nasarataa | They two (f) helped |
| 6 | They (f) | نَصَرْنَ | nasarna | They (f) helped |
| 7 | You (m) | نَصَرْتَ | nasarta | You (m) helped |
| 8 | You two | نَصَرْتُمَا | nasartumaa | You two helped |
| 9 | You all (m) | نَصَرْتُمْ | nasartum | You all (m) helped |
| 10 | You (f) | نَصَرْتِ | nasarti | You (f) helped |
| 11 | You two (f) | نَصَرْتُمَا | nasartumaa | You two (f) helped |
| 12 | You all (f) | نَصَرْتُنَّ | nasartunna | You all (f) helped |
| 13 | I | نَصَرْتُ | nasartu | I helped |
| 14 | We | نَصَرْنَا | nasarnaa | We helped |

### The Present Tense Swatch (Family 1: يَفْعُلُ)

| # | Person | Arabic | Transliteration | Meaning |
|---|--------|--------|----------------|---------|
| 1 | He | يَنْصُرُ | yansuru | He helps |
| 2 | They two (m) | يَنْصُرَانِ | yansuraani | They two help |
| 3 | They (m) | يَنْصُرُونَ | yansuruuna | They help |
| 4 | She | تَنْصُرُ | tansuru | She helps |
| 5 | They two (f) | تَنْصُرَانِ | tansuraani | They two (f) help |
| 6 | They (f) | يَنْصُرْنَ | yansurna | They (f) help |
| 7 | You (m) | تَنْصُرُ | tansuru | You (m) help |
| 8 | You two | تَنْصُرَانِ | tansuraani | You two help |
| 9 | You all (m) | تَنْصُرُونَ | tansuruuna | You all (m) help |
| 10 | You (f) | تَنْصُرِينَ | tansureena | You (f) help |
| 11 | You two (f) | تَنْصُرَانِ | tansuraani | You two (f) help |
| 12 | You all (f) | تَنْصُرْنَ | tansurna | You all (f) help |
| 13 | I | أَنْصُرُ | ansuru | I help |
| 14 | We | نَنْصُرُ | nansuru | We help |

### How to Read the Swatch Rhythmically

Read the "he" column first, going down:
- **نَصَرَ، نَصَرَا، نَصَرُوا** (he, they two, they)

Then the "she" column:
- **نَصَرَتْ، نَصَرَتَا، نَصَرْنَ** (she, they two f, they f)

Then "you":
- **نَصَرْتَ، نَصَرْتُمَا، نَصَرْتُمْ** (you m, you two, you all m)
- **نَصَرْتِ، نَصَرْتُمَا، نَصَرْتُنَّ** (you f, you two f, you all f)

Then "I/we":
- **نَصَرْتُ، نَصَرْنَا** (I, we)

Practice this rhythm until it becomes automatic. The pattern is the same for every Family 1 verb — only the root letters change.

## Chapter 4: Ism Patterns from Verbs

Verbs don't just conjugate — they also produce **Isms** with predictable meanings.

### The Ism Fa'il (اسم الفاعل) — The Doer

The person who does the action.

| Family | Pattern | Example | Meaning |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| 1 | فَاعِل | كَاتِب | Writer |
| 2 | مُفَعِّل | مُعَلِّم | Teacher |
| 4 | مُفْعِل | مُسْلِم | One who submits (Muslim) |
| 5 | مُتَفَعِّل | مُتَعَلِّم | Learner |
| 10 | مُسْتَفْعِل | مُسْتَغْفِر | One seeking forgiveness |

### The Ism Maf'ool (اسم المفعول) — The One Acted Upon

| Family | Pattern | Example | Meaning |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| 1 | مَفْعُول | مَكْتُوب | Written |
| 2 | مُفَعَّل | مُعَلَّم | Taught |
| 4 | مُفْعَل | مُسْلَم | Submitted |

### Other Key Ism Patterns

| Pattern Name | Pattern | Meaning Type | Example |
|-------------|---------|-------------|---------|
| Masdar (مصدر) | Various | The act itself (verbal noun) | كِتَابَة (writing), عِلْم (knowledge) |
| Ism Makan (اسم المكان) | مَفْعَل / مَفْعِل | Place of action | مَسْجِد (place of prostration = mosque) |
| Ism Zaman (اسم الزمان) | مَفْعَل / مَفْعِل | Time of action | مَوْعِد (appointed time) |
| Ism Aala (اسم الآلة) | مِفْعَال / مِفْعَل | Instrument | مِفْتَاح (key — instrument of opening) |

## Chapter 5: Common Quranic Root Families

Here are some of the most frequently occurring roots in the Quran. Study how each root generates a family of related words:

### Root: ع-ب-د (Worship/Serve)

| Word | Family | Meaning |
|------|--------|---------|
| عَبَدَ | 1 | He worshipped |
| يَعْبُدُ | 1 | He worships |
| عَبْد | — | Servant/Slave |
| عِبَادَة | — | Worship (the act) |
| عِبَاد | — | Servants (plural) |
| مَعْبُود | 1 | The one worshipped |

### Root: ر-ح-م (Mercy)

| Word | Family | Meaning |
|------|--------|---------|
| رَحِمَ | 1 | He had mercy |
| يَرْحَمُ | 1 | He has mercy |
| رَحْمَة | — | Mercy |
| رَحْمٰن | — | The Entirely Merciful (intensive) |
| رَحِيم | — | The Especially Merciful (consistent) |
| أَرْحَم | — | Most Merciful (comparative) |
| رَحِم | — | Womb (place of mercy) |

### Root: ه-د-ي (Guidance)

| Word | Family | Meaning |
|------|--------|---------|
| هَدَى | 1 | He guided |
| يَهْدِي | 1 | He guides |
| هُدًى | — | Guidance |
| هَادٍ | 1 | Guide (Ism Fa'il) |
| اِهْتَدَى | 8 | He sought/found guidance |
| مُهْتَدٍ | 8 | One who is guided |

## Key Takeaway

> Sarf is the key that unlocks the vocabulary of the Quran. When you know a word's root and its pattern, you don't just understand one word — you understand an entire family of words. A student of Sarf can encounter a new word and deduce its meaning from its structure.
