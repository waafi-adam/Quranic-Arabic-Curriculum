# Phase 1: The Three Parts of Speech

> "Every single word in the Arabic language — and therefore every word in the Quran — is either an Ism, a Fi'l, or a Harf. There is no fourth option."

---

## Chapter 1: The Ism (Noun)

### What is an Ism?

An **Ism** is a word that refers to a person, place, thing, idea, adjective, or description. It is **not locked in time**. When you say "book" or "beautiful" or "Muhammad," none of these words tell you *when* something happened. That timelessness is the hallmark of an Ism.

**English equivalents:** nouns, adjectives, pronouns, adverbs, proper names.

### How to Spot an Ism

There are four clues. If a word has **any one** of these, it is guaranteed to be an Ism:

| Clue | Arabic Term | Example | Meaning |
|------|------------|---------|---------|
| 1. "Al" at the beginning | ال | **ال**كتاب | The book |
| 2. Tanween at the end | تنوين | كتاب**ٌ** | A book |
| 3. It is preceded by a Harf of Jarr | حرف الجر | **في** المسجد**ِ** | In the masjid |
| 4. It makes sense when you say "the ___" | — | "the mercy" works; "the running" doesn't | — |

> **Quranic Example:**
> بِسْمِ اللهِ الرَّحْمٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ
>
> - **اسم** — Ism (name). Has tanween in its base form. Preceded by بِ (a Harf of Jarr).
> - **الله** — Ism (proper name). Has "Al" embedded in it.
> - **الرَّحْمٰن** — Ism (adjective). Has "Al."
> - **الرَّحِيم** — Ism (adjective). Has "Al."

### Practice: Identifying Isms in the Quran

Open Surah Al-Fatihah. Every word that has **ال** or **tanween** or follows a **Harf of Jarr** is an Ism. Circle them. You should find that the vast majority of words in Al-Fatihah are Isms.

## Chapter 2: The Fi'l (Verb)

### What is a Fi'l?

A **Fi'l** is a word that describes an action **locked in time**. Unlike English, Arabic only has two primary tenses that matter for Quranic study:

| Tense | Arabic Term | Time | Example | Meaning |
|-------|------------|------|---------|---------|
| Past tense | الماضي | Done | كَتَبَ | He wrote |
| Present/Future tense | المضارع | Happening/Will happen | يَكْتُبُ | He writes / He will write |

There is also a **command form** (أمر / Amr): اكْتُبْ — "Write!"

### How to Spot a Fi'l

A Fi'l **cannot** have the Ism clues (no ال, no tanween, not after a Harf of Jarr).

Additionally, look for these patterns:

**Past tense clues:**
- Usually ends in a **fat-ha** pattern: فَعَلَ
- Often has a "he did" meaning at its base

**Present tense clues:**
- Starts with one of the letters: أ ن ت ي (remembered by the word **أنيت** or **نأتي**)
- Example: **يَ**عْلَمُ (He knows), **تَ**عْلَمُ (You know / She knows), **أَ**عْلَمُ (I know), **نَ**عْلَمُ (We know)

> **Quranic Example:**
> خَلَقَ الإِنسَانَ مِنْ عَلَقٍ (Al-'Alaq 96:2)
>
> - **خَلَقَ** — Fi'l Maadi (past tense verb): "He created."
> - Notice: no ال, no tanween, no Harf of Jarr before it.

### Common Quranic Verbs to Recognize

| Past | Present | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| قَالَ | يَقُولُ | He said / He says |
| عَلِمَ | يَعْلَمُ | He knew / He knows |
| جَعَلَ | يَجْعَلُ | He made / He makes |
| كَانَ | يَكُونُ | He was / He is (being) |
| آمَنَ | يُؤْمِنُ | He believed / He believes |

## Chapter 3: The Harf (Particle)

### What is a Harf?

A **Harf** is a word that **does not make sense on its own**. It only makes sense when combined with other words. Think of words like "in," "from," "but," "if," "not" — none of these words mean anything if you just walk up to someone and say them alone.

### The Key Test

Ask: "Does this word make complete sense by itself?"

- "Book" — yes, you can picture it. **Ism.**
- "He wrote" — yes, something happened. **Fi'l.**
- "From" — from what? It's incomplete. **Harf.**

### Important Quranic Huroof (Particles)

| Harf | Meaning | Quranic Example |
|------|---------|----------------|
| فِي | In | فِي قُلُوبِهِم — "In their hearts" |
| مِنْ | From | مِنَ السَّمَاءِ — "From the sky" |
| إِلَى | To/Towards | إِلَى اللهِ — "To Allah" |
| عَلَى | On/Upon | عَلَيْهِمْ — "Upon them" |
| بِ | By/With | بِسْمِ — "With/By the name" |
| لِ | For/To | لِلَّهِ — "For Allah" |
| وَ | And | وَالسَّمَاءِ — "And the sky" |
| ثُمَّ | Then | ثُمَّ يَتُوبُ — "Then He accepts repentance" |
| إِنَّ | Certainly | إِنَّ اللهَ — "Certainly Allah" |
| لَا | No/Not | لَا إِلٰهَ — "No god" |

### Huroof are Not Isms

This is a common confusion. Huroof:
- Never have "Al" (ال)
- Never have tanween
- Never follow another Harf of Jarr

They are the "glue" of the Arabic sentence.

## Chapter Summary

| | Ism | Fi'l | Harf |
|---|-----|------|------|
| **Meaning** | Person, place, thing, idea, description | Action locked in time | Connector/helper word |
| **Time?** | No | Yes | No |
| **Makes sense alone?** | Yes | Yes | No |
| **Example** | كِتَاب (book) | كَتَبَ (he wrote) | فِي (in) |
| **Clues** | ال, tanween, after Harf of Jarr | Past tense pattern, أنيت prefix | No Ism clues, incomplete meaning |

## Key Takeaway

> Every word in the Quran is an Ism, Fi'l, or Harf. Once you can classify words, you have taken the first and most important step toward understanding Allah's words directly.
