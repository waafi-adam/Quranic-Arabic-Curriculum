# Part 3: The Sentence (الجملة)

> "Arabic has only two types of sentences. Once you know how each one works, you can analyze any ayah in the Quran."

**Prerequisite:** Complete Part 1 (Nahw) and Part 2 (Sarf).

---

## Chapter 1: The Jumla Ismiyya (الجملة الاسمية) — The Noun Sentence

### The Big Idea

A Jumla Ismiyya is a sentence that **begins with an Ism**. It has two parts:

| Part | Arabic Term | Role | Status |
|------|------------|------|--------|
| Subject | مُبْتَدَأ (Mubtada) | What you're talking about | Always **Raf'** |
| Predicate | خَبَر (Khabar) | What you're saying about it | Always **Raf'** |

**There is no "is" in Arabic.** The sentence is complete without a verb.

### Basic Structure

> **اللَّهُ غَفُورٌ**
>
> - اللَّهُ = Mubtada (Raf') — "Allah"
> - غَفُورٌ = Khabar (Raf') — "forgiving"
> - Full meaning: "Allah **is** forgiving."

The "is" exists in the meaning but not in the Arabic text. This invisible "is" is one of the most important concepts in Quranic grammar.

### Rules of the Mubtada

1. The Mubtada is **always in Raf'** status.
2. The Mubtada is almost always **definite** (has ال, is a proper name, or has a pronoun).
3. The Mubtada usually comes **first**.

### Types of Khabar

The Khabar (predicate) can be one of four things:

#### Type 1: Single Word
> الْكِتَابُ **جَدِيدٌ** — "The book is **new**."

#### Type 2: Jarr-Majroor Fragment
> الْحَمْدُ **لِلَّهِ** — "All praise is **for Allah**."
>
> - الحمد = Mubtada (Raf')
> - لله = Khabar (Jarr-Majroor fragment acting as the predicate)

#### Type 3: Idhafah Fragment
> مُحَمَّدٌ **رَسُولُ اللَّهِ** — "Muhammad is **the Messenger of Allah**."
>
> - مُحَمَّدٌ = Mubtada
> - رَسُولُ اللَّهِ = Khabar (Idhafah fragment)

#### Type 4: Entire Sentence
> اللَّهُ **يَعْلَمُ** — "Allah **knows**."
>
> - اللَّهُ = Mubtada
> - يَعْلَمُ = Khabar (a whole verb sentence acting as predicate)

### Quranic Examples of Jumla Ismiyya

> **ذٰلِكَ الْكِتَابُ لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ** (Al-Baqarah 2:2)
>
> "That is the Book — no doubt in it."
>
> - ذٰلِكَ = Mubtada ("That")
> - الْكِتَابُ = Khabar ("the Book")
> - لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ = Second sentence: لا + رَيْبَ (Nasb) + فِيهِ (Jarr-Majroor)

> **وَاللَّهُ عَلِيمٌ حَكِيمٌ** (An-Nisa 4:26)
>
> "And Allah is All-Knowing, All-Wise."
>
> - اللَّهُ = Mubtada (Raf')
> - عَلِيمٌ = Khabar #1 (Raf')
> - حَكِيمٌ = Khabar #2 (Raf') — a Mubtada can have multiple Khabars

## Chapter 2: The Effect of إنَّ on the Jumla Ismiyya

When **إنَّ** (or her sisters) enters a Jumla Ismiyya, it changes the structure:

| Without إنَّ | With إنَّ |
|-------------|----------|
| Mubtada (Raf') + Khabar (Raf') | Ism of إنَّ (**Nasb**) + Khabar of إنَّ (Raf') |

> **Without:** اللَّهُ رَحِيمٌ — "Allah is Merciful."
> **With:** إِنَّ اللَّهَ رَحِيمٌ — "Certainly, Allah is Merciful."
>
> Notice: اللَّه**ُ** (damma) became اللَّه**َ** (fat-ha). The Khabar رَحِيمٌ stays in Raf'.

### All of إنَّ's Sisters

| Sister | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| إِنَّ | Certainly | إِنَّ اللَّهَ غَفُورٌ |
| أَنَّ | That... | عَلِمْتُ أَنَّ اللَّهَ غَفُورٌ |
| كَأَنَّ | As if... | كَأَنَّ الشَّمْسَ قَرِيبَةٌ |
| لٰكِنَّ | But/However... | لٰكِنَّ اللَّهَ رَحِيمٌ |
| لَيْتَ | I wish... | لَيْتَ الشَّبَابَ عَائِدٌ |
| لَعَلَّ | Hopefully... | لَعَلَّكُمْ تُفْلِحُونَ |

## Chapter 3: The Jumla Fi'liyya (الجملة الفعلية) — The Verb Sentence

### The Big Idea

A Jumla Fi'liyya is a sentence that **begins with a verb**. It has up to three parts:

| Part | Arabic Term | Role | Status |
|------|------------|------|--------|
| Verb | فِعْل (Fi'l) | The action | N/A (verbs don't have status) |
| Doer | فَاعِل (Fa'il) | Who did it | Always **Raf'** |
| Object | مَفْعُول بِهِ (Maf'ool bihi) | Who/what it was done to | Always **Nasb** |

### Basic Structure

> **خَلَقَ اللَّهُ السَّمَاوَاتِ**
>
> - خَلَقَ = Fi'l (past tense verb: "created")
> - اللَّهُ = Fa'il (Raf': "Allah" — the doer)
> - السَّمَاوَاتِ = Maf'ool bihi (Nasb: "the heavens" — the object)
> - Full meaning: "Allah created the heavens."

### The Fa'il (Doer)

**Rule 1:** The Fa'il is always in **Raf'** status.

**Rule 2:** The Fa'il can be **visible** or **hidden**.

#### Visible Fa'il
> كَتَبَ **الطَّالِبُ** — "**The student** wrote."
> The Fa'il (الطَّالِبُ) is explicitly stated.

#### Hidden Fa'il (Pronoun embedded in the verb)
> **كَتَبُوا** — "**They** wrote."
> The و at the end IS the Fa'il — it's a pronoun meaning "they."

> **كَتَبْتُ** — "**I** wrote."
> The تُ at the end is the Fa'il pronoun.

This is why the Sarf conjugation tables (Part 2, Chapter 3) are so important — each conjugation already has the Fa'il built in.

### The Maf'ool Bihi (Object)

**Rule:** The Maf'ool bihi is always in **Nasb** status.

> قَالَ **الْحَقَّ** — "He said **the truth**."
> الْحَقَّ = Nasb (fat-ha) = Maf'ool bihi

Not every verb sentence has a Maf'ool bihi. Some verbs are **intransitive** (they don't take an object):

> ذَهَبَ الرَّجُلُ — "The man went."
> There is no object — you can't "go" something.

### Quranic Examples of Jumla Fi'liyya

> **قَالَ رَبُّكَ لِلْمَلَائِكَةِ** (Al-Baqarah 2:30)
>
> "Your Lord said to the angels..."
>
> - قَالَ = Fi'l (past: "said")
> - رَبُّكَ = Fa'il (Raf': "your Lord") — Idhafah: رَبُّ + كَ
> - لِلْمَلَائِكَةِ = Jarr-Majroor (detail: "to the angels")

> **يُحِبُّ اللَّهُ الْمُحْسِنِينَ** (Al-Baqarah 2:195)
>
> "Allah loves those who do good."
>
> - يُحِبُّ = Fi'l Mudari' (present: "loves")
> - اللَّهُ = Fa'il (Raf': "Allah")
> - الْمُحْسِنِينَ = Maf'ool bihi (Nasb: "those who do good" — Sound Masc. Plural, Nasb = ينَ)

## Chapter 4: Verb-Doer Agreement

When a verb comes **before** its doer, there are specific agreement rules:

### Rule 1: The verb is always SINGULAR
Regardless of whether the Fa'il is dual or plural, the verb stays singular.

> **قَالَ الرِّجَالُ** — "The men said." (NOT قَالُوا الرِّجَالُ)
> The verb قَالَ is singular even though الرِّجَالُ is plural.

### Rule 2: The verb matches the Fa'il in GENDER
> **قَالَ الرَّجُلُ** — "The man said." (masculine verb)
> **قَالَتِ الْمَرْأَةُ** — "The woman said." (feminine verb — note the تْ)

### Summary Table

| Fa'il | Verb Form | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Singular masculine | فَعَلَ | ذَهَبَ الرَّجُلُ |
| Singular feminine | فَعَلَتْ | ذَهَبَتِ الْمَرْأَةُ |
| Dual masculine | فَعَلَ (singular!) | ذَهَبَ الرَّجُلَانِ |
| Dual feminine | فَعَلَتْ (singular!) | ذَهَبَتِ الْمَرْأَتَانِ |
| Plural masculine | فَعَلَ (singular!) | ذَهَبَ الرِّجَالُ |
| Plural feminine | فَعَلَتْ (singular!) | ذَهَبَتِ النِّسَاءُ |

## Chapter 5: Extra Details in the Sentence

Beyond the core Fa'il and Maf'ool bihi, sentences often include additional details. These are mostly in **Nasb** status:

### Types of Extra Details (المفاعيل والحال)

| Type | Question Answered | Status | Example |
|------|------------------|--------|---------|
| Maf'ool bihi (Object) | What? | Nasb | كَتَبَ **الرِّسَالَةَ** |
| Maf'ool fihi (Time/Place) | When/Where? | Nasb | صُمْنَا **يَوْمًا** |
| Maf'ool lahu (Reason) | Why? | Nasb | قُمْتُ **احْتِرَامًا** |
| Maf'ool mutlaq (Emphasis) | How intensely? | Nasb | ضَرَبَ **ضَرْبًا** شَدِيدًا |
| Haal (State) | In what state? | Nasb | جَاءَ **رَاكِبًا** (He came **riding**) |

> **Quranic Example of Haal:**
> وَمَا خَلَقْنَا السَّمَاءَ وَالْأَرْضَ وَمَا بَيْنَهُمَا **لَاعِبِينَ** (Al-Anbiya 21:16)
>
> "And We did not create the heaven and earth and what is between them **playing**."
> لَاعِبِينَ = Haal (Nasb — ينَ ending of Sound Masculine Plural)

## Chapter 6: Putting It All Together — Full Ayah Analysis

Let's do a complete grammatical breakdown of an ayah using everything we've learned:

> **إِنَّ اللَّهَ يُحِبُّ الْمُحْسِنِينَ** (Al-Baqarah 2:195)

### Step 1: Identify the sentence type
This starts with إِنَّ, which means it was originally a Jumla Ismiyya that إِنَّ entered.

### Step 2: Identify each word

| Word | Part of Speech | Role | Status | Properties |
|------|---------------|------|--------|------------|
| إِنَّ | Harf | Harf of Nasb | — | Forces next Ism to Nasb |
| اللَّهَ | Ism | Ism of إنَّ | **Nasb** (fat-ha) | Singular, Masc., Definite, Proper |
| يُحِبُّ | Fi'l | Present tense verb (Family 4) | — | Root: ح-ب-ب |
| الْمُحْسِنِينَ | Ism | Maf'ool bihi (object of يُحِبُّ) | **Nasb** (ينَ) | Sound Masc. Plural, Masc., Definite |

### Step 3: Identify fragments
- إِنَّ + اللَّهَ = Harf of Nasb + Ism fragment
- يُحِبُّ الْمُحْسِنِينَ = Jumla Fi'liyya (verb sentence) acting as the Khabar of إنَّ

### Step 4: Sarf analysis
- يُحِبُّ: Root ح-ب-ب, Family 4 (أَحَبَّ / يُحِبُّ — "to love"). The يُ prefix = "he" (3rd person masculine).
- الْمُحْسِنِينَ: Root ح-س-ن, Family 4 Ism Fa'il pattern (مُفْعِل → مُحْسِن = "one who does good"). Plural with ينَ = multiple doers.

### Step 5: Translation
"Indeed, Allah loves those who do good."

Do this for 10 minutes daily. Within weeks, the grammar of the Quran will begin to feel natural.

## Key Takeaway

> Arabic has only two sentence types: the Jumla Ismiyya (Subject + Predicate, both in Raf') and the Jumla Fi'liyya (Verb + Doer in Raf' + Object in Nasb). Every ayah in the Quran is built from one or both of these structures, combined with the fragments you learned in Phase 3. You now have the complete toolkit to begin understanding the Quran in its original language.
